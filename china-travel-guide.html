Shanghai

Overview

Shanghai is China's most populous city and a global financial center. Known as the "Paris of the East" in the 1920s and 1930s, it has evolved into a futuristic metropolis while preserving its historical charm.

With a history dating back over 1,000 years, Shanghai became a major trading port in the 19th century, leading to a unique blend of Chinese and Western influences that is still evident in its architecture and culture.

Cultural Highlights

  • Unique fusion of Chinese and Western architectural styles in the Bund
  • Traditional Shanghainese opera and music
  • Dynamic contemporary art scene
  • Historic water towns in the surrounding area

Beijing

Overview

Beijing, China's capital, has been a political and cultural center for over 800 years. It served as the capital for several dynasties, leaving behind a wealth of historical treasures.

Today, Beijing is a fascinating mix of ancient traditions and modern development, with skyscrapers standing alongside imperial palaces and historic hutongs (narrow alleyways).

Cultural Highlights

  • Imperial heritage from Ming and Qing dynasties
  • Traditional Peking opera performances
  • Ancient hutong neighborhoods with traditional courtyard homes
  • World-class museums including the Palace Museum

Zhangjiajie

Overview

Zhangjiajie is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its unique sandstone pillars, formed over 380 million years. These towering peaks, often shrouded in mist, create a magical landscape.

The area was largely isolated until the 1980s when it began developing as a tourist destination. It gained international fame after serving as inspiration for the floating mountains in the movie Avatar.

Cultural Highlights

  • Traditional Tujia and Miao ethnic minority cultures
  • Folk dances and music performances
  • Ancient villages preserving traditional lifestyles
  • Unique local crafts including batik and embroidery

Sichuan

Overview

Sichuan is a province in southwestern China known for its fertile plains, dramatic mountain ranges, and rich cultural heritage. Often called the "Land of Abundance," it has a long history of agriculture and trade.

The province is geographically diverse, from the Sichuan Basin with its mild climate to the Himalayan mountains in the west. This diversity has shaped a unique culture with distinct traditions.

Cultural Highlights

  • Famous Sichuan opera with its unique face-changing technique
  • Traditional tea culture and tea houses
  • Diverse ethnic minorities including Tibetans, Yi, and Qiang
  • Ancient irrigation systems like Dujiangyan that have been in use for over 2,000 years

Chengdu

Overview

Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan province, is one of China's most livable cities, known for its relaxed pace of life despite being a major metropolitan area with over 16 million people.

With a history dating back over 2,000 years, Chengdu has long been an important cultural and economic center in southwest China, famous for its fertile lands and agricultural productivity.

Cultural Highlights

  • World-famous giant panda breeding research base
  • Traditional tea houses where locals gather to socialize
  • Ancient Jinli Street with traditional architecture and crafts
  • Chengdu opera featuring unique musical styles

Food Recommendations

Chinese food varies dramatically by region. Here are the must-try dishes in each location.

Shanghai Food

  • Xiaolongbao (小笼包)

    Steamed soup dumplings with pork or crab filling

  • Shengjianbao (生煎包)

    Pan-fried pork buns with crispy bottoms

  • Hairy Crab (大闸蟹)

    Seasonal delicacy, especially in autumn

  • Sweet and Sour Ribs (糖醋排骨)

    Tangy-sweet pork ribs

Beijing Food

  • Peking Duck (北京烤鸭)

    Roast duck with crispy skin, served with pancakes

  • Zhajiangmian (炸酱面)

    Noodles with soybean paste and vegetables

  • Jiaozi (饺子)

    Dumplings, often filled with pork or vegetables

  • Lamb Hot Pot (涮羊肉)

    Thinly sliced lamb cooked in a simmering broth

Sichuan & Chengdu Food

  • Mapo Tofu (麻婆豆腐)

    Spicy tofu with minced pork in chili oil

  • Kung Pao Chicken (宫保鸡丁)

    Spicy stir-fried chicken with peanuts

  • Hot Pot (火锅,海底捞 Hai Di Lao is the best!)
    - But come to Dalongyi NZ!!!

    Spicy broth with various ingredients to cook

  • Dan Dan Noodles (担担面)

    Spicy Sichuan noodles with sesame paste

Zhangjiajie Food

  • Tujia Sticky Rice (土家族糯米饭)

    Glutinous rice with local ingredients

  • Braised Pork with Cabbage (扣肉)

    Layered pork belly with preserved cabbage

  • Mountain Bamboo Shoots (山竹笋)

    Fresh local bamboo shoots prepared various ways

  • Stir-fried Fern (炒蕨菜)

    Wild fern vegetable stir-fried with garlic

  • Grilled Fish (烤鱼)

    Local river fish grilled with spices

  • Rice Cake (糍粑)

    Glutinous rice cake, often sweet

  • Sour Radish Fish Soup (酸萝卜鱼汤)

  • Mountain Honey (山蜂蜜)

    Pure honey from local mountains

Useful Apps for China Travel

These apps will help you navigate, communicate, and make the most of your trip in China.

How to Download Apps in China (iOS Guide)

  1. Step 1: Open Settings on your iPhone and tap on your Apple ID at the top
  2. Step 2: Select "Media & Purchases" then "View Account"
  3. Step 3: Tap "Country/Region" and choose "China mainland"
  4. Step 4: Agree to the terms and conditions
  5. Step 5: For payment method, select "None"
  6. Step 6: Enter a Chinese address (you can use a hotel address)
  7. Step 7: Open the App Store - you'll now see the Chinese store
  8. Note: You can switch back to your original store after your trip using the same method

WeChat (微信)

China's all-in-one app for messaging, payments, social media, and more.

How to Use:

  • • Create an account with your phone number
  • • Add contacts by scanning QR codes
  • • Link a Chinese bank card for payments
  • • Use "Moments" for social updates
  • • Book taxis, order food, and pay bills

Alipay (支付宝)

Popular mobile payment app accepted almost everywhere in China.

How to Use:

  • • Register with your phone number
  • • Use the "International Version" for foreign users
  • • Link international credit cards
  • • Generate your payment QR code to pay
  • • Use for shopping, dining, and transportation

Didi Chuxing (滴滴出行)

China's main ride-hailing app, similar to Uber.

How to Use:

  • • Set your pickup and drop-off locations
  • • Choose your ride type (economy, comfort, etc.)
  • • Pay through the app with WeChat/Alipay
  • • Use the English version for easy navigation
  • • Can book taxis, private cars, and even buses

Gaode Maps (高德地图)

Essential mapping app with detailed directions for China.

How to Use:

  • • Switch to English in settings
  • • Search for locations in English or Chinese
  • • Get walking,公交 (bus), and subway directions
  • • Find nearby restaurants, shops, and attractions
  • • Download offline maps for data-free use

Meituan (美团)

Everything app for food delivery, restaurant bookings, and more.

How to Use:

  • • Order food delivery to your location
  • • Book restaurant tables with discounts
  • • Buy movie tickets and attraction passes
  • • Use for grocery delivery and pharmacy orders
  • • Pay with WeChat or Alipay

Pleco

Powerful Chinese-English dictionary and translation app.

How to Use:

  • • Look up words by typing or drawing characters
  • • Use the camera to translate menus and signs
  • • Listen to correct pronunciation
  • • Save useful phrases for quick access
  • • Includes essential travel vocabulary lists

Travel Tips

Essential advice to make your trip to China smoother and more enjoyable.

Visa & Documentation

  • Ensure your visa is valid for all cities you plan to visit
  • Carry a printed copy of your visa and passport at all times
  • Register with your country's embassy upon arrival
  • Some attractions require ID - carry a photocopy of your passport

Internet & Communication

  • Consider purchasing a local SIM card for data and calls
  • Many hotels offer free VPN services for accessing international sites
  • Download offline maps before your trip
  • WeChat is essential for communication with locals

Money & Payments

  • Carry some cash - while digital payments are widespread, small vendors may not accept them
  • Link your international cards to Alipay for easier payments
  • Notify your bank before traveling to avoid card blocks
  • ATMs are widely available in cities but may have withdrawal limits

Transportation

  • China's high-speed rail is efficient and connects major cities
  • Book train tickets in advance during peak seasons
  • Use Didi for convenient taxi services in cities
  • Subway systems in major cities are affordable and easy to use

Dining Etiquette

  • Tipping is not expected in most restaurants
  • Many restaurants serve food family-style, shared among the table
  • Use chopsticks correctly - don't stick them upright in rice
  • It's polite to try a little of every dish served to you

Health & Safety

  • Drink only bottled water to avoid stomach issues
  • Carry any necessary medications in original packaging
  • Be aware of your surroundings in crowded areas to prevent pickpocketing
  • Save local emergency numbers in your phone
